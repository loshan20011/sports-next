Cricket

What is it?

  • Cricket is a bat-and-ball sport that is played between two teams of eleven players on a field that has a wicket at each end made up of two bails balanced on three stumps.
  • From one end of the 22-yard pitch, the bowler launches the ball with a straight arm in an effort to remove the batsman by striking the wicket at the other end, enticing the batsman to hit the ball into the air and into the hands of a fielder, or committing one of a variety of other mistakes. In such a way when a batsman is removed, it is called a wicket.
  • By hitting the ball to the field boundary or far enough away from the fielders that he or she can sprint to the other end of the pitch before the ball can be returned, the batsman tries to protect the wicket with the bat and score runs, the game's currency.
  • When all the wickets are taken or after an agreed period, the roles are reversed. Which means, the team that is batting then bowls and the team that is bowling then bats.
  • The team that scores the most runs wins.
  • There are 2 types of cricket. One is soft ball cricket which is played using a tennis ball and the other is hard ball cricket which is played using a hard ball called a leather ball.

Is it for me?

  • Cricket is an outdoor sport which is played by people of all ages, genders and background.
  • Mostly it is for people who are interested in being active, being part of a team and to enjoy the thrill of competition.

What to expect when I start?

  • Cricket is sport which is played mostly in outdoors but it can also be played in indoor grounds (example: futsal indoor courts).
  • Before playing cricket, you need to understand the basic rules of the game. It can be quite complicated at first but once you get the hang of it, it will be easy to understand.
  • Cricket calls for a variety of athletic abilities, such as agility, endurance, and hand-eye coordination. Drills, workouts, and match simulations will be used to help you hone these talents.
  • This will provide a great opportunity to meet new people, make new friends, stay fit and healthy.

Effective sport Winning Strategies

  • To win a cricket match you need to have a good team. You need to have a good captain who can lead the team and make the right decisions.
  • Then you need to be effective in the field. You need to have efficient bowlers and a good fielding team who can catch the ball and throw it to the wicket.
  • Your team must be constist of good batsmen who can score runs and can defend the wicket.
  • The key to winning a cricket match is to have a good team and to play as a team.

Costs in sport

  • In soft ball cricket, you need to have a tennis ball and a tennis bat, a wicket set(when playing professional matches you need 2 sets). These items are quite cheap. This mode can be played either wearing shoes, slippers or barefoot.
  • In hard ball cricket, you need to have a leather bat, a leather ball, a wicket set(when playing professional matches you need 2 sets), a pair of gloves, a helmet, a pair of pads, a pair of shoes, a pair of socks and other guarding eqiupments like chest guard. These items are quite expensive.

Cricket ground and Pitch dimensions

  • In a standarad cricket ground diameter changes between 137m and 150m. The length of the pitch is 22 yards. Boundary line can not exceed 82.29m from the center of the pitch.

Refereeing

  • The on-field umpires, who are in charge of making game-related decisions, act as referees in cricket. A cricket match typically has two on-field umpires who stand at opposite end of the pitch. A third umpire helps them out by watching the game on television and reviewing some of the calls made by the on-field umpires.
  • The on-field umpires are in charge of determining whether a fielder has taken a catch or not, whether a ball is a no-ball or a wide, and whether a batsman is out or not. They also maintain a record of the number of overs bowled and make sure the game is played in line with the regulations.
  • When the on-field umpires are unsure on a call or when a team wants a review of a call, the third umpire steps in. The third umpire then examines the incident's video and delivers a judgment based on what they saw. Run-out and stumping decisions are assisted by the third umpire as well.
