INTERNATIONAL SOCCER

  • In terms of both players and viewers, football is the most watched sport in the world. Modern football originated in Britain in the 19th century. Since before medieval times, “folk football” games had been played in towns and villages according to local customs and with a minimum of rules.
  • Football has 17 laws that govern the equipment, field of play, participant behaviour, and determination of results. The International Football Association Board, which is made up of representatives from FIFA and the four British football organisations, has the authority to change the laws.
  • People in Europe and South American nations embraced the sport and made it their own. Several clubs play in international competitions that are being held throughout Europe at the same time in order to win a coveted cup.

GAMEPLAY

  • On a huge grass pitch that is rectangular in shape, two teams play football. On either side of the field's length, there are two goal posts. Both sides compete for possession of the ball by tackling one another and attempting to kick the ball into their opponent's goal post.
  • The team for which they are playing is indicated by the shorts, socks, and number on the players' jerseys. It is necessary to wear shoes and shin protectors. Goalkeepers must be identifiable from all other players and match officials, and both sides must wear jerseys that may be easily distinguished from one another.
  • Only in the restricted region surrounding the goal post may the goalie stop the ball with his or her hands. The other players must kick the ball and pass it to teammates in order to participate. A football team's goal is to outscore their opponent and win the game. After the ball crosses the goal line, a goal is scored.

FOOTBALL PITCH

  • The penalty area is a square in front of the goal that is 44 yards (40.2 meters) wide and stretches 18 yards (16.5 meters) onto the field. The goal is an 8-yard (7.3-meter) wide by 8-foot (2.4-meter) high frame with a net backing it. The playing surface (pitch) should measure 100–130 yards (90–120 metres) long by 50–100 yards (45–90 metres) broad. Professional football stadiums should be 105 meters long, 68 meters broad, and cover an area of little over 7 square kilometers.

RULES AND REGULATIONS

  • Each game lasts 45 minutes, divided into two halves with a 15-minute break in between.
  • A player touching the ball is considered to have committed "handball," which results in a penalty for the opposing team and excludes the goalie.
  • The goal may be scored from any part of the body aside from your hands as long as the ball is in the other team's net and fully in, signifying it has successfully crossed the line.
  • In the event that a player is injured or the game goes into extra time, each team is permitted a maximum of three substitutes apiece.
  • There must be a referee and two helpers for every game. The referee's duties include overseeing fair play, ruling on penalties, and issuing yellow and red cards..
  • A yellow card serves as a warning; a player would be removed from the field of play after receiving two of them. A player in this circumstance cannot be replaced if this is the case.
  • A "throw in," in which a player tosses the ball back into play, can be used to play a ball that has left the field of play (gone out of play).
  • An attacking player receives a goal kick (such as a penalty) if the ball leaves play on their baseline; however, if the ball comes from a defender, it is a corner kick.
  • When a player receives two yellow cards and then makes a mistake, they are given a red card.

THE BALL

  • The ball must be 14.5–16 ounces (410–450 grams) in weight, 14.5–17.5 inches (68–70 cm) in circumference, and coated with leather or another appropriate material.

FOOTBALL TOURNAMENTS

  • The World Cup, which is managed by FIFA, is the greatest and most renowned international football competition. Once every four years, this tournament is held. In order to qualify for this competition, between 190 and 200 national teams are competing.
  • Other than FIFA, The most well-known and prominent competitions are the continental championships, which are run by the confederations of these continents.
  • European Championship (UEFA)
  • Copa America (CONMEBOL)
  • Africa Cup of Nations (CAF)
  • Asian Cup (AFC)
  • Confederations Cup (CONCACAF)
  • OFC Nations Cup (OFC)
